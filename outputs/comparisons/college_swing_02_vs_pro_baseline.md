## Interpretation Guardrails

- The professional baseline is not an ideal swing model.
- Values are pose-derived estimates, not ground-truth biomechanics.
- Camera angle, clip trimming, frame rate, and pose detection quality affect the metrics.
- Contact is a proxy because the bat and ball are not tracked.
- This report is useful for system evaluation and exploratory comparison, not final coaching advice.

## Summary

The test swing produced valid core metrics and could be compared against the professional baseline. Head movement and peak-hand-speed-to-contact timing fell within the middle 50% of the professional sample. Hand path distance was below the professional middle 50%, but this should be interpreted carefully because the detected swing window was shorter than the professional baseline.

The experimental metrics show why visual inspection and data-quality checks remain important. Shoulder angle change appears affected by angle wraparound, and the start-to-contact timing metrics are sensitive to clip trimming, frame rate, and event detection behavior. Overall, this report demonstrates that the pipeline can process an unseen swing and generate an honest pro-baseline comparison with documented limitations.
